Understanding Phishing

Phishing is a deceptive method used by cybercriminals to pose as legitimate sources in order to trick individuals into revealing private data like login credentials, financial information, or personal details.

How This Simulation Was Designed

The test email you received was carefully crafted to resemble common phishing attacks. Here's what made it convincing:

  • The sender’s email resembled a trusted domain like security-alerts@securenet.com.
  • It used an urgent subject like “Action Required: Account Issue Detected”.
  • The content mentioned suspicious activity to provoke a quick response.
  • The link led to a fake login portal.
  • You were asked to enter your credentials, just as real attackers would attempt.

Spotting Phishing Red Flags

  • Double-check sender details – Is the domain legitimate?
  • Be cautious of pressure tactics – “Immediate action required” is often a scam signal.
  • Hover to inspect links – Don’t click until you’re sure of the destination.
  • Avoid unexpected file attachments – Especially from unfamiliar senders.
  • Watch for spelling and grammar issues – Poor writing is often a giveaway.
  • Don’t enter sensitive info through email links – Reputable companies won’t ask for that.

What to Do If You Suspect a Phishing Email

  • Don’t click on any suspicious links or open attachments.
  • Report the message to the SecureNet Security Team: security@securenet.com
  • Delete the message from your inbox.
  • If you’ve already interacted, inform IT immediately and update your passwords.
